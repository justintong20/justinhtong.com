About

I like building things that take a hit. Whether it's a modular pylon for the MQ-9 Reaper or a 3-lb combat robot designed to survive arena impacts, I'm happiest when I'm working through the full design-build-test cycle — CAD to layup to competition floor.

At SDSU I've spent most of my time in the shop and the sim. I run FEA in ANSYS, design in SolidWorks, and have gotten my hands dirty with composite fabrication (VARTM), 3D printing, and CNC. My senior design work with General Atomics taught me how to hold a design to MIL-STD requirements and a 1.5 factor of safety while keeping weight down — and how to present that work to working engineers who don't have time for fluff.

Outside of engineering, I race triathlons competitively — 12th at USAT Age Group Nationals in 2025 and a 4:56 at Santa Cruz 70.3. I'm also an Eagle Scout, which is probably where the instinct to lead small teams and stay organized comes from.

I'm looking for a role in aerospace, defense, or manufacturing where precision matters and the work has purpose.

Projects

MQ-9 Reaper Modular Pylon

Senior Design — General Atomics

Engineered a modular payload pylon for the MQ-9 Reaper UAV, increasing hardpoint connections from 1 to 4. Ran static structural FEA across multiple load cases, designed composite layups using laminate theory, and fabricated structural components via VARTM. Delivered a full-scale design package and half-scale model to General Atomics engineers.

SolidWorks ANSYS FEA Composites / VARTM MIL-STD

3-lb Combat Robot

ASME Design Team — Project Lead

Designed and 3D printed a 3-lb combat robot optimized for additive manufacturing constraints and impact loading. Led a multidisciplinary team through full design-build-test cycles for CSUN and National Havoc Robot League competitions. Selected materials and manufacturing methods balancing cost, lead time, and structural requirements.

SolidWorks 3D Printing CNC DFM

FSAE Composite Undertray

Aztec Electric Racing

Contributed to the research, design, and fabrication of a carbon fiber composite undertray for SDSU's Formula SAE electric vehicle. Also developed an instrument panel in SolidWorks compliant with FSAE hardware and safety regulations.
